Extending Zeeman-Doppler Imaging to Pulsating Magnetic Stars

Modelling V2052 Oph using the PIMMS code

Chloe Gutteridge, Coralie Neiner, and Claude Catala
CHFT Users Meeting 2025, 26th - 28th of May, 2025

Introduction

Zeeman-Doppler Imaging (ZDI) is a technique that inverts a series of spectropolarimetric data allowing the reconstruction of stellar magnetic fields, which provides valuable insights into their strength, geometries, and time evolution. However, ZDI normally assumes that stellar line profile variations (LPVs) are due solely to rotation - an assumption that breaks down for pulsating stars, where oscillations introduce additional Doppler shifts.

We present the Pulsation-Informed Magnetic Mapping of Stars (PIMMS) code, an adaptation of the ZDIpy code of Folsom et al. (2018). By modelling the pulsation modes identified with spectroscopic, spectropolarimetric, and/or photometric observations, PIMMS creates a time-dependent surface velocity map which accounts for all causes of Doppler shifts of local lines. This allows the causes of LPVs associated with rotation, i.e. magnetic fields and spots, to be accurately assigned locations on the stellar surface preventing errors in the magnetic field strength and geometry; and spot locations, relative brightness, and sizes. This is crucial for obtaining the correct stellar and magnetic properties.

The PIMMS Code

$$p_{ij}(v,t) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}\exp\left(-\frac{(v_{ij}(t)-v)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right)$$

PIMMS models local line profiles as Gaussians with time-dependent Doppler shifts \(v_{ij}(t)\), dependent on the rotational and pulsational velocities. We choose to use the pulsation velocity derivation of Aerts et al. (2010).

Mode parameters \(\ell\), \(m\), and the frequency \(f\) must be provided for each mode, while phase at \(t=0\), velocity amplitude and the ratio between tangential and radial displacements are fitted.

Conclusion

Pulsation velocities must be considered when performing Zeeman-Doppler imaging, and this is now possible with the PIMMS code. Understanding the surface magnetic fields of hot main sequence pulsators will allow us to perform magneto-asteroseismology, i.e. asteroseismology with the additional constraint of the magnetic field. This has previously been performed for V2052 Oph, which observed at inhibition of core overshooting (Briquet et al. 2012). Such studies will allow us to better understand the main sequence lifetimes of hot stars, which is currently poorly understood due to uncalibrated prescriptions for magnetic fields in evolutionary models. stars. With ESPaDOnS at CFHT (and other spectropolarimeters), we are currently obtaining data of SPB, β Cep, and δ Sct variables for use with PIMMS, and with the goal of performing magneto-asteroseismology.
